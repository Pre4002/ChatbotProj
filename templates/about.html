About the National Rail Museum

Explore the rich history of India's railway system at the National Rail Museum, where technology, heritage, and culture come to life.

History

The National Rail Museum, established in 1977, showcases the evolution of rail transport in India. Located in New Delhi, it displays vintage trains, engines, and carriages, representing key milestones in the railway sector.

Peak Hours

The museum is busiest from 11:00 AM to 2:00 PM on weekends and public holidays. Visit during off-peak hours for a quieter experience.

Holidays

  • Republic Day (January 26)
  • Independence Day (August 15)
  • Gandhi Jayanti (October 2)
  • Diwali (Date varies)
  • Holi (Date varies)
  • Christmas (December 25)

Location

The National Rail Museum is located in Chanakyapuri, New Delhi, near the diplomatic enclave. Easily accessible by metro, buses, and taxis. The nearest metro station is Shadipur Metro Station on the Blue Line.
